Cartograms - Lukas Maximilian Masopust

Image source: Cartogram of Organic Farming Land Proportion Across All Countries

What is being shown?
This visualization shows a so-called cartogram, which is a map with an encoded variable distorting the geographical information in order to highlight said variable. In this specific cartogram, the variable that causes the geometric distortion is the area of a countries organic farming land.
Is the visualization interactive or static?
This visualization is static.
What are some of the design choices and visualization techniques used?
In this visualization, colors in the same range encode for a specific region. E.g. western Europe is represented by red and only slight differences in gradients are used to make countries discernable. The distortion clearly outlines that e.g. Australia has a large proportion on certified organic farming land when compared to other countries, such as the U.S..
What, in your opinion, is the rationale behind the design choices?
The choice to color code a certain region, such as Europe, within the same color range helps blending individual contries into bigger areas and help giving the user a view of the bigger picture and allows for cross contintent comparison.
Since humans are very familiar with using maps, using a standard representation of the earth helps with connecting the information we want to showcase through the cartogram (in this case organic farming area) with their knowledge of geography.
Do you think this is a good visualization? Give a short critique on why or why not.
I think cartograms are a great way of focussing a user's attention on a very specific variable with concern to geographical data. Building upon the prior knowledge of maps, the distortion easily shows a specific variable.
